    frame

    Frame is an augmented reality art gallery. We provide galleries and artists the ability to share and experience art from all around the world in the comfort of your home. Music has Spotify and Movies has Netflix and a billion other streaming services, but the average doesn't have a platform like that. That's what we set out to fix.

    I interviewed over 50 artists about their struggles or success as an artist and how they navigated the art world. Part of what I learned is how importnant a place just for them to display their art. Instagram and Etsy have their good points, but a private space just for them, like a gallery, is priceless. Everyone should be able to share their art with anyone.

    I designed and wrote the copy for the company website, I still run the company instagram and worked with my friends Tony Leon and Leo Cantoran on the prototype, logo, and other graphic work for the company. I also manage public outreach with galleries and artists.

    The logo is a great example of the growth and exploration that I went through. I wanted something that was colorful and ambiguous, both a beautiful 2D shape as well as a 3D box, because it highlights the purpose of the app: turning a 2D experience into a 3D one. The colors I chose came from the emotions I wanted to bring out: yellow for warmth, pink for passion, and purple for curiosity and imagination. After I decided my colors, I searched for when these colors occur naturally together. The answer I found was black opal, but instead of distinct colors, I transformed it into a gradient.

    scholar cloud

    Scholar Cloud was an assignment in my Entreprenuership class, it was dreamed up to be a scholarship management system for universities and schools, where scholarships are actually easily available, organized, and formally integrated into schools. Not an afterthought for schools or students.

    We interviewed a dozen students of varying age and grade, and interviewed a few professionals who worked with YMCA and counselors of different high schools, and from our open format of our questions, we were able to brainstorm a product that can help solve administration and making scholarships more available.

    I completed the Scholar Cloud prototype with input from my peers and from follow up interviews. I had to restructure the prototype to better serve both the instituion and student sides of things.
